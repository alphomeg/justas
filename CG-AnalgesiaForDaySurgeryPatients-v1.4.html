Clinical Guideline
ANALGESIA FOR DAY SURGERY PATIENTS

SETTING Surgical Head and Neck – Day Surgery Patients

FOR STAFF Recovery staff and Ward staff discharging day surgery patients

PATIENTS Adult patients undergoing Surgical operations as a planned Day Case procedure

GUIDANCE

Preoperative
Use preoperative analgesia where you can.

Give a loading dose of paracetamol according to the loading dose policy. See below.

Give a non-steroidal anti-inflammatory (NSAID) agent or consider a COX II inhibitor unless contraindicated, (caution in: peptic ulcer disease, renal impairment {Chronic Kidney Disease stage ≥ 2}, age >70)

Postoperative analgesia
Follow the acute pain guidelines (see document management system)

If mild pain
If patient has not had paracetamol give now and consider a NSAID or COX II as above. In addition consider tramadol or codeine.

If moderate to severe pain:
Give intravenous strong opioid: fentanyl, oxycodone or morphine (as per IV opioid administration guidelines cf acute pain guidelines) until pain score is less than 5. Then consider tramadol or codeine to maintain analgesia. If anticipated ongoing moderate pain consider one dose modified release strong opioid (5-10mg oxycontin). Consider antiemetics. Ensure regular paracetamol and NSAIDs as above.

If a patient is unexpectedly requiring a large dose of strong opioid in recovery then consider whether they will be comfortable to go home on the oral medication prescribed and obtain review by the surgeon and anaesthetist and discuss timing of appropriate discharge. Consider adding oral morphine as a take home drug along with a laxative. The patient’s ability to go home will depend on several factors including nature of the operation and timing of the patient on the operating list.
