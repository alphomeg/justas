In all suspected stroke patients (new admissions or inpatients) clarify time of onset and consider whether the patient is suitable for thrombolysis and or thrombectomy
If your patient has ongoing stroke symptoms and symptoms started within 12 hours or they have just woken up with the symptoms call 2222 and ask for the thrombolysis teamand arrange an urgent CT head and CT angiogram intracranial scan
o Refer to AGWS acute ischaemic stroke reperfusion guidelines (see stroke admission proforma)
If your patients symptoms have completely resolved and lasted <24 hours consider a TIA
o Refer to TIA protocol available on DMS
ALL suspected stroke patients need to be admitted directly to A515. Do not admit to AMU/other ward before speaking to the stroke team
All stroke patients should be admitted to the stroke unit within four hours from hospital arrival
If the patient is not suitable for thrombolysis or thrombectomy and is a new admission inform the stroke team of the patient immediately via the stroke ASAP phone (x27816) For inpatient strokes not suitable for thrombolysis or thrombectomy please call the stroke ASAP phone (x27816) if urgent advice is required and complete a Medway referral

(Out of hours discuss with the medical registrar on call)

CT Head




All stroke patients should be imaged within an hour including out of hours
A CT Head scan should be arranged immediately for patients in the following circumstances:
o Being considered for reperfusion therapy (thrombolysis and or thrombectomy) o known bleeding tendency/on anticoagulation
o Depressed or deteriorating consciousness ( Ascertain need for anaesthetic/ITU support for any unstable patients)
o Severe headache at symptom onset
o Head injury

VTE Prophylaxis






Complete VTE assessment on the drug chart for all patients
Use thigh length IPC stockings in stroke patients for 30 days or until the person is mobile or discharged and if not contraindicated (see guidance)
Avoid LMWH as VTE prophylaxis in haemorrhagic stroke or for the first two weeks in ischaemic strokes unless discussed with stroke consultant
Review VTE decision at 24 hours and at two weeks in all stroke patients

Oxygen

Patient should receive oxygen supplementation only if saturations <95% and there is no contraindication (i.e. COPD patients with a target 88-92%)

Fluids





All patients need a swallow screen (only complete if trained to do so) Patients should remain NBM until screened.

Start IV fluids (1 litre of normal saline 0.9% over eight hours) if NBM (avoid dextrose), check electrolytes before starting if possible and adjust accordingly. Take caution if active or known history of heart failure

Glycaemic control




The aim is for blood glucose 4-11mmol/l
Consider sliding scale if needed (liaise with senior clinician)

Ischaemic stroke patients (clinical stroke and CT normal or confirmed acute ischaemic stroke)

Antiplatelet / anticoagulation advice

First 24 hours





Stop all anticoagulants (po/IV/SC) ( If significant clinical indication i.e. recent PE, mechanical heart valve then discuss with senior first and /or discuss with stroke
consultant)
o N.B. for TIA patients, (symptoms fully resolved) continue anticoagulation and discuss with stroke team if concerns
o See Flow chart from ESC re restarting or initiating anticoagulation after an ischaemic stroke (Fig 1)
Give first dose of antiplatelet treatment immediately - options as follows:
1. Aspirin 300mg po/pr unless aspirin intolerant (see below)
2. If all of:
a) very minor stroke ( NIHSS ≤ 3)
b) 48hours or less from onset of symptoms and
c) Bleeding risk not excessive

Also give one dose of Clopidogrel 300mg in addition to one dose of Aspirin 300mg

Ongoing treatment





If first dose given was option 1 (Aspirin 300mg), continue Aspirin 300mg for two weeks then switch to Clopidogrel 75mg longer term (or switch to Clopidogrel on discharge if sooner)
If first dose given was option 2 (Clopidogrel 300mg and Aspirin 300mg stat loading doses) continue patient on Clopidogrel 75mg and Aspirin 75mg for four weeks then stop Aspirin and continue Clopidogrel 75mg alone longer term

If Aspirin intolerance: Clopidogrel 75mg alone (po/ng) is an alternative. If Clopidogrel intolerance: use Aspirin 300mg for two weeks and then continue 75mg Aspirin long term

Starting/restarting anticoagulation

Consider starting/restarting anticoagulation in patients after ischaemic strokes with confirmed AF. The timing of this depends upon stroke severity. Please see flow chart below (Fig 1)

In patients with new AF, discuss and document the risk/benefits of anticoagulation conversation with patient and /or carer, family in the medical notes

If starting/restarting anticoagulation, stop the antiplatelet. However in some cardiology patients both an anticoagulant and an antiplatelet is required (ie recent stent) therefore if concern please discuss with the cardiology team.

Carotid/ Vertebral artery dissection







Patients should be managed as per normal protocol.

Patients should be considered for acute thrombolysis and thrombectomy as per normal guidelines.

Request a CT and CTA if dissection is suspected
Patients with acute ischaemic stroke due to dissection should be treated with either an anticoagulant or an antiplatelet for at least three months (please liaise with stroke team for advice)
o First line would be to start aspirin 300mg until advice is sought

Blood Pressure management

If symptoms ≤6 hours ago and blood pressure ≥150mmHg systolic treat urgently to lower the blood pressure to a target of 140mmhg for at least seven days unless:





The haematoma is very large and death is expected
A structural cause for the haematoma is identified
Immediate surgery to evacuate the haematoma is planned

If symptoms >6 hours ago there is no clear evidence around BP lowering please discuss with stroke consultant/senior for advice

Consider further imaging to identify cause of the bleed (liaise with radiology consultants/stroke consultants before requesting)

Please see ‘parenteral management of hypertension in stroke guidelines’.

Driving advice in all stroke and TIA patients

Group 1 License:





Stop driving for one month.

If residual symptoms after one month inform DVLA and don’t drive.

If more than one event within one month stop driving for at least three months and inform the DVLA

Group 2 License:

 Stop driving for one year and inform the DVLA

All groups: Seizures


