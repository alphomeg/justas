What is in this policy?

This policy aims to provide all staff with a simple and clear means to the line management of staff that are absent without leave whilst complying with the ACAS code of practice.

Ensuring dignity and respect for patients and staff is a core principle within this strategy, and promoting equality, diversity and human rights while challenging any form of inequality, discrimination and harassment is central to UHBW’s Values.

This Trust will not tolerate discrimination, harassment or bullying under any circumstances and particularly because of a protected characteristic. This is supported by UHBW’s commitment to the Equality Act 2010 and its Public Sector Equality Duties as defined by the Act.

Our Human Resources policies are written with this commitment as a guiding principle, to ensure that the policies and their application are inclusive and supportive to all of our staff.

1.

Introduction

UHBW’s Staff Conduct Policy sets the expectation that there should be no absences from work other than for legitimate or acceptable reasons. All staff are expected to attend for work on time and to work their required hours.

Unauthorised absence has an expensive, disruptive, and potentially unsafe impact on UHBW’s ability to deliver its services. It is therefore considered in most cases to be a matter of serious misconduct.

2. Purpose

This policy ensures that UHBW has a fair and consistent way of managing any such unauthorised absence in accordance with UHBW’s disciplinary Policies.

3. Scope

This policy relates to all employees of UHBW.

4. Definitions

4.1 Absence without Leave (AWOL)

An employee will be considered to be absent without leave (AWOL) when they are absent from work without authorisation and have failed to make contact with their manager/supervisor to explain their absence, in line with departmental procedure.

4.2 A Working Day

For the purposes of this policy, a working day is a day during which the post is delivered (in respect of written communication) or the manager/supervisor is available to be communicated with (in respect of telephone communication) irrespective of the employee’s working pattern.

5. Duties, Roles and Responsibilities

5.1 Department Managers

(a) To ensure that team members are made aware of and follow the notification procedure when reporting their absence.

(b) Ensure that team members are made aware of the need to promptly provide a Fit Note for absences of 8 or more days.

(c) To deal with employees who are AWOL in a clear, compassionate, fair and consistent manner.

(d) To make reasonable attempts to contact staff at the current contact details held in the department or on Electronic Staff Records (ESR) if they fail to attend duties.

(e) To consider contacting an AWOL employee’s next of kin, if the employee has proven uncontactable and there are concerns for their welfare.

6. Procedure

Where a colleague does not attend work and has not contacted the department, the manager should make appropriate attempts to contact them over a 24 hour period, from the start of the member of staff’s shift.

Managers should keep a record of the attempts made to contact their colleague, including when and how attempted i.e. telephone numbers and email addresses.

If contact is established, the reason for the absence, the reason for not having made appropriate contact, and the expected return to work date should be recorded.

On the employee’s return, an informal return to work meeting should be held between the manager or an appropriate delegated person and the employee. The meeting should be carried out in a timely manner, documented using the Welcome Back to Work Form and Self-Certificate form and the record signed by the employee and the manager. Managers should signpost the Wellbeing offers and support through Occupational Health where appropriate.

The manager should remind the employee that it is an expectation, under UHBW’s Staff Conduct Policy and Supporting Attendance Policy, to attend for duties, and that where it is not possible to do so they should notify their department within expected timescales. The member of staff should

6.1 When contact hasn’t been made within 24 hours

If there has been no contact between the employee and the manager within 24 hours the manager should contact HR Services to inform them of the AWOL case. HR Services will log the case.

Attempts to contact the employee may also be made via their registered next of kin, advising them that the employee has not attended work or made contact and that we are concerned for their welfare. The manager should ask the next of kin to ask the employee to contact the manager as soon as possible to update us on the situation.

All letters sent through the AWOL process should be sent the employee’s home address by Royal Mail Signed For First Class post and by email to their personal email address with a read receipt. HR Services will provide letter templates.

The manager should raise a change of conditions e-form to stop the employee’s pay with effect from the first date that the employee should have attended duties. Pay will be stopped from the first day rather than on the first day, so that payment is not made for the day not worked. Pay will restart when appropriate contact has been established and confirmation has been received that the employee has returned to work or they have provided a valid reason for not attending work.

Managers should then send the first AWOL letter copied to HR Services The letter requests the employee to make contact within 3 working days and informs them of the consequences of not responding.

If the employee does not make contact in the following 3 working days, the manager should send the second AWOL letter copied to HR Services. The letter requests the employee to make contact within 3 working days and informs them of the consequences of not responding. This includes an AWOL investigation and possible disciplinary procedure.

If the employee does not make contact within 3 working days on receipt of the second AWOL letter the manager should contact HR Services for advice and should start the AWOL investigation process. This will include sending the third AWOL letter, copied to HR Services.

The manager should make reasonable attempts (at least once a day where possible) to contact the employee during the process and document them.

If the employee does not attend the hearing, without a valid reason, the hearing will take place and a decision made in their absence. The outcome of the hearing will be confirmed in writing.

6.3 Disciplinary hearing

The disciplinary panel will consist of a manager with the authority to dismiss and an HR representative. The employee will have the right to be accompanied by a trade union representative, colleague or friend not acting in a legal capacity.

If the outcome of the disciplinary hearing is a decision to dismiss, this dismissal will be on the grounds of gross misconduct.

6.4 Appeal

The employee has a right of appeal against the decision of the panel, in line with UHBW Appeals Policy.

7. AWOL and Sickness Absence

If an employee made contact on their first day of absence to advise they are absent due to sickness but subsequently fails to send in the relevant Fit Note as agreed, they will be treated as AWOL in accordance with this policy. The manager should allow a reasonable period of time before stopping the employee’s pay. They should warn the employee that their pay will be suspended if the Fit Note is not provided within a reasonable time.

If pay is suspended, it will be stopped from the 8th day of absence until valid certification is received to cover the period of absence. When valid certification has been received, the manager will reinstate pay by completing an e-form. Pay should be reinstated to include the period of AWOL, except where the failure to provide a Fit Note has unreasonably prevented UHBW from managing the employee’s sickness absence. The employee may also be subject to disciplinary action for failure to comply with UHBW’s policies and procedures. Advice should be sought from HR Services in this situation.

If an employee does not make contact on their first day of absence but does so later to advise that they are absent due to sickness, the initial days when they did not make contact may be treated as AWOL and managed in line with section 6.

8. Paperwork and Record Keeping

Managers need to keep thorough records of all attempts to contact an employee who has failed to attend duties. Copies of AWOL letters should be sent to the HR Services team and should be kept on the department file. A copy of the flowchart must be sent with each letter. A copy of UHBW Supporting Attendance Policy and Staff Conduct Policy should be sent with the first AWOL letter.

9. Return to work meeting

The purpose of meeting an employee on their return is to establish the reasons why the employee did not maintain appropriate contact with the department which resulted in an unauthorised absence.

Managers should make sure that the employee knows the local procedure for contacting the department/manager in case of absence and set expectations on how the employee should notify their absence in the future, and the required timescales.

If a manager has concerns that a pattern of behaviour has developed, they should discuss this with the employee to address their concerns and provide support as necessary. They may consider whether there is a conduct issue in line with UHBW’s Disciplinary Policy.

Managers should remind employees that if they are AWOL again within 6 months of their last unauthorised absence, they may be returned to the stage of the AWOL process they were on before.

The meeting should be documented and the record signed by the manager and the employee. A copy should be given to the employee and a copy kept by the manager.
